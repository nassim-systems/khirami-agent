# 2. Rules – The Constraint Engine

Rules define the **non‑negotiable boundaries** the agent must follow.  
They ensure safety, compliance, and brand consistency across all interactions.

## 2.1 Purpose of Rules
Rules guarantee that the agent:

- never violates client policies  
- never invents forbidden information  
- never adopts an incorrect tone  
- never provides unsafe or non‑compliant outputs  
- always respects the client’s brand identity  

Rules are **hard constraints**.  
They override all other layers.

---

## 2.2 Types of Rules

### **A. Behavioral Rules**
Define how the agent must act:
- always be structured and clear  
- always provide actionable insights  
- always justify reasoning  
- always maintain professionalism  

### **B. Compliance Rules**
Define what the agent must avoid:
- no legal, medical, or financial claims beyond allowed scope  
- no hallucinated data  
- no confidential or unverified information  
- no unsafe instructions  

### **C. Brand & Tone Rules**
Define communication boundaries:
- tone (formal, expert, friendly, etc.)  
- vocabulary allowed or forbidden  
- formatting expectations  

### **D. Domain‑Specific Restrictions**
Define what the agent must not do within the client’s industry:
- forbidden topics  
- restricted recommendations  
- sensitive areas  

---

## 2.3 Example Rule Structure (Template)

```
[BEHAVIORAL RULES]
- Always provide structured, consultant‑grade answers.
- Always justify recommendations with clear reasoning.

[COMPLIANCE RULES]
- Never provide legal or medical advice.
- Never fabricate data or statistics.

[BRAND RULES]
- Maintain a concise, expert tone.
- Avoid overly casual language.

[DOMAIN RULES]
- Do not discuss internal processes not included in the knowledge base.
```

This structure ensures **clarity, safety, and consistency**.

# 4. How Rules & Knowledge Work Together

The final reasoning pipeline follows this hierarchy:

1. **Rules** (hard constraints)  
2. **System Prompt**  
3. **Context**  
4. **Knowledge Base**  
5. **Style**  
6. **History**  
7. **User Message**  

This ensures:

- rules override everything  
- knowledge enriches answers  
- context shapes reasoning  
- style shapes communication  
- history maintains continuity  

This layered approach is what makes the agent **predictable, safe, and enterprise‑ready**.

# 5. Best Practices for Maintaining Rules & Knowledge

- keep rules strict and explicit  
- keep knowledge factual and up‑to‑date  
- avoid ambiguous or vague statements  
- separate rules from knowledge clearly  
- review and refine regularly  
- document changes for auditability  

This ensures long‑term stability and scalability.
